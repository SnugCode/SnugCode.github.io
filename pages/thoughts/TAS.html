The Avenue School

A capstone redesign of The Avenue School's web presence, balancing accessibility, clarity, and the practical realities of building for a real institution.

The Brief

The Avenue School serves students with diverse learning needs. The existing site made it difficult for families to find essential information quickly. The goal was a redesign that prioritised clarity above everything else, no unnecessary complexity, no buried navigation.

Design Decisions

Early versions leaned too heavily on visual variety. The revision stripped that back: consistent type scale, high contrast ratios throughout, and a navigation structure built around the questions families actually ask. Every layout choice was weighed against how it read on a mid-range mobile device.

What Changed Across the Build

The information architecture shifted twice during the project as feedback revealed how different stakeholders, parents, staff, and prospective families, prioritised different content. That tension shaped the final hierarchy more than any individual design preference.

What I Took Away

Designing for institutions means designing for people who are often stressed or time-poor. Speed to the right information matters more than aesthetic ambition. The project reinforced that restraint is a design decision, not a compromise.
